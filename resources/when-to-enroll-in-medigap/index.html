When to enroll in Medigap

The enrollment window matters more than the plan

People shopping for a Medigap plan usually focus on which plan letter to choose. That matters, but when you enroll can matter even more, because your rights as an applicant change depending on timing.

Your Medigap Open Enrollment Period

Your Medigap Open Enrollment Period is a one-time, six-month window that begins the month you are both 65 or older and enrolled in Medicare Part B.

During this window, insurance companies generally cannot:

  • Turn down your application because of your health history
  • Charge you a higher premium because of health conditions
  • Make you wait for coverage of pre-existing conditions beyond limited, defined periods

This protection is called guaranteed issue. It exists at this strength during this window and in a small number of other defined situations — which is why the timing deserves your attention.

What happens after the window closes

Once your Open Enrollment Period ends, you can still apply for a Medigap plan at any time of year — there is no annual deadline for Medigap applications. But the insurance company may use medical underwriting. That means it can review your health history, ask about current conditions and medications, and decide whether to accept your application and at what premium.

Underwriting is not an automatic no. Many people pass it. But it introduces uncertainty that did not exist during your enrollment window, and the outcome depends on your health at the time you apply.

Guaranteed issue rights after the window

Federal rules define certain situations where you regain the right to buy specific Medigap plans without underwriting, even after your Open Enrollment Period has passed. These are called guaranteed issue rights, and they are generally triggered by losing other coverage through no fault of your own — for example, an employer or union plan that ends, or an insurance company that stops offering your coverage.

These rights come with specific conditions and time limits, and they typically apply to specific plan letters rather than every plan. If your coverage situation is changing, it is worth finding out whether a guaranteed issue right applies to you before the deadline attached to it passes.

If you are turning 65 soon

A practical timeline: start learning about your options two to three months before your Part B coverage begins. That gives you time to understand the plan letters, compare premiums between companies, and enroll early in your window rather than at the end of it. Medigap coverage can begin as soon as your Medicare coverage does, so there is no advantage in waiting.

If your window has already passed

Do not assume the answer is no. Underwriting standards differ between companies, and guaranteed issue situations are more common than many people realize. Asking costs you nothing, and the answer tells you exactly where you stand.

Where to get free help

For general Medicare questions, you can call 1-800-MEDICARE or contact your local SHINE office (Serving Health Insurance Needs of Elders), Florida’s free counseling program for seniors.
